Chhole Bhature

Ingredients:

    For Chhole:

  • 1 cup dried white chickpeas (or 3 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 3 cups water
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ¼ teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (optional)
  • ½ or 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur powder)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala

    • For Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup lukewarm water
  • Ghee or oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. Soak and Cook Chickpeas: Rinse the chickpeas, soak them overnight, and cook them in a pressure cooker or pot with water and salt until tender. Drain the water and set aside.
  2. Make Chhole Masala: Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamoms, cloves, and bay leaves until aromatic. Grind them into a fine powder. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, asafoetida (if using), and amchur powder. Mix well.
  3. Prepare Chhole: Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add the slit green chili. Add the cooked chickpeas, Chhole Masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 10-15 minutes or until the flavors meld together.
  4. Make Bhature: Mix flour, salt, and baking powder. Gradually add lukewarm water to form a smooth dough. Knead for 5-7 minutes. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Fry Bhature: Divide the dough into 4-5 equal portions. Roll out each portion into a thin circle. Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan and fry the bhature until golden brown and puffed up. Drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve: Serve the Chhole with Bhature, garnished with chopped cilantro and a dollop of butter, if desired.
